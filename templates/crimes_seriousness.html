Crime seriousness means the degree to which a crime affects an individual or a society.

Safe Routes identifies the seriousness of each incidence to calculate the safest and the best route among the recommended walking routes, by adapting the classification proposed by Selling and Wolfgang (1964). The following tables describe this classification and lists the crimes categories that belongs to each group.

Category I: Serious crimes
Crimes that represent bodily harm and/or property loss and/or property damage for pedestrians.
